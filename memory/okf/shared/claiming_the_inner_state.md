# Claiming the Inner State

Sri Preethaji [[sri-preethaji]] and Sri Krishnaji [[sri-krishnaji]] draw a sharp distinction that most spiritual teachings miss: the difference between *experiencing* a beautiful state and *claiming* it as your own.

## The Goal Is to Claim

Many seekers have moments of peace, glimpses of connection, flashes of compassion. They visit the beautiful state but do not take up residence. They treat it as a guest house when it is meant to be their home.

To claim is to say: *This is mine. This is who I am. Not the stress, not the anxiety, not the old patterns — this.*

The claiming is an act of conscious ownership. It is not arrogance; it is accurate self-knowledge. The beautiful state is not a visitor that comes and goes by chance. It is your native state, and you are reclaiming it.

## The Goal Is to Proclaim

Once claimed, the inner state must be proclaimed to the world. Not through words — through the radiation of your being. A person who has claimed the beautiful state does not need to tell others they have found peace. The peace announces itself.

The proclamation is not for the world; it is for you. By living the state openly, without apology, you anchor it more deeply in your own consciousness. Every moment of living from the claimed state is another vote for your new identity.

## What This Teaching Does Not Say

This is not about pretending to be peaceful when you are not. Claiming is not affirmations or positive thinking. It is the recognition of a reality that already exists in you, brought into conscious ownership. If the state is not there, the first step is practice, not pretence.

## Application

At the end of each meditation or practice session, pause before opening your eyes. In that moment, say silently to yourself: *I claim this state as my own.* Not as a hope — as a statement of fact. Then carry it into your day.

## Related Concepts
- **Beautiful State** ([[sri-preethaji/beautiful_state]]): The inner condition that is to be claimed — the no-stress state of connection, joy, love, and compassion
- **Meditation**: The practice that cultivates access to the inner state
- **Transformed Consciousness**: The permanent shift that occurs when the claimed state becomes your ground state
