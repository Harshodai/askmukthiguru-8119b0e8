# Finding Truth in Chaos

# Finding Truth in Chaos

## Summary
In this teaching, Sri Preethaji and Sri Krishnaji address the fundamental question of self-realization amid the chaos of worldly existence. They emphasize that our true nature—the Self or Atman—is distinct from the transient experiences we encounter in daily life. This understanding forms the foundation for finding inner truth even when external circumstances appear chaotic or turbulent.

## Key Teachings
- The fundamental truth is that we are the true Self, completely distinct from our experiences. Sri Preethaji and Sri Krishnaji teach that this distinction is essential for spiritual liberation.
- The Self, referred to as Atman, is pure consciousness—unchanging, eternal, and untouched by the fluctuations of worldly life.
- There exists a fundamental separation between the permanent "I" (the Self/Atman) and the transient "it" of our worldly existence. This teaching points to the illusion of identifying with external circumstances rather than our inner truth.

## Quotes
> "The truth is that we are the true Self, distinct from the experience." — Sri Preethaji & Sri Krishnaji

> "This Self, or Atman, is pure consciousness." — Sri Preethaji & Sri Krishnaji

> "It is fundamentally separate from the transient 'it' of our worldly existence." — Sri Preethaji & Sri Krishnaji

## Related Concepts
- **Atma (Atman)**: The true Self or soul in Hindu philosophy; pure consciousness that is eternal and distinct from the body and mind.
- **Moksha**: Spiritual liberation; the release from the cycle of rebirth achieved through recognizing one's true nature as Atman rather than identifying with transient worldly experiences.
