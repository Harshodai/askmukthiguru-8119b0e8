# The Inner Truth of Suffering

*Teacher: [[sri-preethaji]]*

About eight years before the teaching, Sri Krishnaji met with an accident at
Big Bear Lake in California. While in the waters, he realized the root cause of
all human suffering: what keeps suffering alive for days, months, and years
after a stressful event is over is **obsessive, self-centric thinking** — a
total, incessant preoccupation with oneself.

## What triggers vs. what chains

Past and present circumstances, other people, and their behavior can *trigger*
stress, but they do not *keep us chained* in unhappiness. The trigger is
external; the chain is internal — our own self-centric preoccupation.

## The shape of suffering

In suffering we are preoccupied with:
- injustice done to us
- pleasure that was denied us
- opportunities that did not come our way
- love that was withheld from us

The entire thinking collapses into "me, me, me." We are not concerned with
finding solutions to the problem at hand.

## Nomi as illustration

Throughout the fable, Nomi is preoccupied — with his rightness, with his guilt
over allowing a wrong, with justifying that he was right to ignore the
suffering woman. He is not concerned about the situation, the woman, her son,
or his friend Yesmi.

## The liberation

Realizing that all lingering suffering is self-centric preoccupation is the
most powerful antidote for any form of suffering. When you see it — not only do
you walk out of stressful states, but there is a great release of energy and
you awaken to a sense of power, connection, freedom, and purpose.
