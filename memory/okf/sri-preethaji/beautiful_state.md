# The Beautiful State

Sri Preethaji [[sri-preethaji]] teaches that every human being lives in only two states: a
**stressful state** and a **no-stress state**. The no-stress state is called
the *beautiful state*, because that is how life feels when lived free of
stress.

Anger, fear, hurt, loneliness, and frustration are all stressful states. A
beautiful state is a state of connection, joy, love, compassion, vitality, and
passion. If you are not in a beautiful state, your default state is stress.

## The two monks (Yesmi and Nomi)

Yesmi and Nomi are two monks returning to their monastery. Yesmi helps a woman
cross a swollen river and continues his walk in peace. Nomi, bound by ideas of
duty and virtue, is agitated before, during, and after the event. Yesmi
represents the beautiful state — present in action, total in departure. Nomi
represents the stressful state — lost in self-centric thinking.

## The root of suffering

All emotional suffering arises from **obsessive, self-centric thinking** — a
total, incessant preoccupation with oneself. In suffering we are preoccupied
with injustice done to us, pleasure denied, opportunities missed, love
withheld. This preoccupation separates us from others and fuels war, conflict,
and division of every kind.

## Three failing paths

1. Living in stressful states and plummeting into hurting inner states.
2. Escaping stress via temporary dopamine highs — when the dopamine plateaus,
   we are left emotionally dry and empty.
3. Replacing inconvenient beliefs with convenient ones — none of these has the
   power to lift us out of the gravitational pull of suffering.

The way out is the way of **inner truth**: seeing that all lingering suffering
is self-centric preoccupation. Seeing this releases energy and awakens power,
connection, freedom, and purpose.
