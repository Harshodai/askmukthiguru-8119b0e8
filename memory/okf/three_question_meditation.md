# Three-Question Meditation

A short practice Sri Preethaji leads at the close of the Beautiful State
teaching. Meditation is not about a religion; it is about being human — about
returning to a beautiful state.

## Steps

1. **Close your eyes.** Bring attention to your breath. Inhale and exhale deep
   and slow.
2. **Observe yourself — question 1:** Are you in a stressful state or are you in
   a beautiful state? Just observe; do not try to change anything.
3. **Observe yourself — question 2:** Are you engaged with your past or your
   future, or are you in the present?
4. **Observe yourself — question 3:** Are you preoccupied with yourself, or are
   you in a state of connection?
5. **Imagine a flame** glowing in the middle of your forehead. Bring a gentle
   smile to your face.
6. **Open your eyes**, look into the eyes of the person sitting next to you.
   Wish from the bottom of your heart that the other person lives in a
   beautiful state. If comfortable, you may hug the other.

## Why it works

The three questions surface the very axis of the teaching: state (stress vs.
beautiful), time (past/future vs. present), and self (preoccupation vs.
connection). Simply observing each — without trying to change it — loosens the
grip of self-centric thinking and lets the beautiful state return on its own.
