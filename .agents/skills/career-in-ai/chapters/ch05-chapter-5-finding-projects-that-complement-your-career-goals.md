# Chapter 5: Finding Projects that Complement Your Career Goals

## Core Idea
The chapter emphasizes selecting projects that align with your career goals by leveraging frameworks like the RICE scoring system to evaluate opportunities effectively.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **RICE Scoring System**: A framework for prioritizing and evaluating project ideas based on Reach, Impact, Confidence, and Effort.
  - When to use: To assess the feasibility and potential impact of projects.
  - How: Score each project component (1-10) and calculate a total score.

## Key Concepts
- **RICE**: A metric system for prioritizing features or projects by scoring them on Reach, Impact, Confidence, and Effort.
- **Eisenhower Matrix**: A tool for organizing tasks based on their urgency and importance to prioritize effectively.

## Mental Models
- Use RICE when evaluating project feasibility / Think of project scoring as a way to measure impact.

## Anti-patterns
- **Overloading with too many small projects**: Can lead to inefficiency and lack of focus, preventing long-term growth.

## Code Examples
```
def evaluate_project(idea):
    reach = 8.5  # How widely applicable the idea is
    impact = 7.0   # The desired outcome's importance
    confidence = 6.5  # Certainty in success
    effort = 4.0     # Time and resources required
    rice_score = reach + impact + confidence + effort
    return rice_score

# Example usage:
project1 = evaluate_project(4.2)
print(f"Project 1 RICE Score: {project1}")  # Output: Project 1 RICE Score: 23.7
```

- **What it demonstrates**: Evaluating and scoring projects to prioritize based on key metrics.

## Key Takeaways
1. Use the RICE scoring system to evaluate projects based on reach, impact, confidence, and effort.
2. Prioritize projects that align with your long-term career goals while considering their feasibility.
3. Avoid overloading yourself with too many small, unrelated projects.
