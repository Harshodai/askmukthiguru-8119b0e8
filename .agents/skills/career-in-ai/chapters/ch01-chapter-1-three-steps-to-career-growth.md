# Chapter 1: Three Steps to Career Growth

## Core Idea
The chapter outlines three essential strategies for achieving sustainable career growth, emphasizing actionable principles and practical frameworks.

## Frameworks Introduced  
- **PDCA (Plan-Do-Study-Act)**: A structured approach to continuous improvement.  
  - When to use: For refining skills or processes in a professional setting.  
  - How: Plan objectives, execute actions, study outcomes, and act on improvements.  

- **TRIZ (Topological Methodology for Invention)**: A problem-solving framework rooted in mathematics and logic.  
  - When to use: To tackle complex challenges in career transitions or innovation-driven roles.  
  - How: Identify contradictions, generate solutions, validate ideas, and implement outcomes.  

## Key Concepts  
- **The 80/20 Rule**: Prioritize tasks by focusing on the 20% that yields 80% of results.  
- **Mental Models**: Frameworks like PDCA and TRIZ provide structured approaches to problem-solving and career advancement.

## Mental Models  
- Use PDCA when you need a systematic method for skill development or process improvement.  
- Think of TRIZ as a tool for solving complex problems by identifying underlying contradictions in your approach.

## Anti-patterns  
- **Overgeneralization**: Applying broad principles without considering the specific context can lead to ineffective strategies.  

## Code Examples  
```
# Example: Applying the 80/20 Rule
def prioritize_tasks(tasks):
    # Sort tasks by impactiveness
    sorted_tasks = sorted(tasks, key=lambda x: x['impact'], reverse=True)
    # Focus on top 20% with 80% results
    return sorted_tasks[:20]

# This demonstrates how to apply the 80/20 Rule to prioritize career growth tasks.
```
