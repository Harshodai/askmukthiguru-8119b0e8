## Core Idea
This chapter explores the multimodal capabilities of large language models (LLMs) by examining how BLIP-2 processes visual and textual inputs together to generate descriptive captions and engage in chat-based prompting.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **BLIP-2**: A state-of-the-art multimodal model that bridges vision and text through learned representations.
  - When to use: For tasks requiring understanding of both visual and textual information.
  - How: By preprocessing images and text together, then generating multimodal outputs.

## Key Concepts
- **Tokenization**: The process of converting input text into tokens for model processing. BLIP-2 tokenizes inputs with special characters replaced for interpretability.
- **Visual prompts**: Descriptive text generated by models to guide image generation tasks.
- **Chat-based prompting**: Interactive questioning style that allows models to engage in conversational multimodal reasoning.

## Mental Models
- Use BLIP-2 when you need an LLM to process and generate outputs based on both visual and textual inputs. Think of BLIP-2 as a tool for creating descriptive captions or engaging chatbots.

## Anti-patterns
- **Ignoring multimodal capabilities**: Failing to leverage models' ability to handle multiple modalities can result in less effective solutions compared to specialized multimodal architectures.
