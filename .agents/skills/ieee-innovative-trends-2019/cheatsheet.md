## **Decision Tables**
| **Quick Reference Rule** | **Example of a Decision Table** |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Use decision tables for evaluating multiple criteria. | A table that lists all possible scenarios (conditions) and their corresponding outcomes to make informed decisions. |

### Example:
| Condition 1 | Condition 2 | Outcome |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| True        | False       | Decision A |
| False       | True        | Decision B |
| True        | True        | Decision C |

---

## **Comparison Matrices**
| **Quick Reference Rule** | **Example of a Comparison Matrix** |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Use comparison matrices to compare options based on multiple factors. | A matrix that evaluates alternatives against criteria using pairwise comparisons or scoring systems. |

### Example:
| Criteria \ Alternative | Criterion 1 | Criterion 2 | Criterion 3 |
|-------------------------|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| Alternative A            | High         | Medium       | Low          |
| Alternative B            | Medium       | High         | High         |
