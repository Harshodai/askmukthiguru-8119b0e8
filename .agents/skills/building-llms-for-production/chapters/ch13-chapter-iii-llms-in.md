# Chapter III: LLMs in Practice  
## Core Idea  
Large Language Models (LLMs) can generate hallucinations due to biased training data and limited mechanisms for verifying factual accuracy, leading to potentially misleading information spread.

## Frameworks Introduced  
- **Temperature Parameter Tuning**:  
  - When to use: To control response diversity. Lower temperatures increase predictability; higher temperatures encourage creativity.  
  - How: Adjust temperature values (e.g., 0.7 for conservative responses) to reduce hallucinations.  

- **Retriever Architectures**:  
  - When to use: To anchor LLM outputs in relevant documents, ensuring responses are grounded in reality.  
  - How: Implement retriever modules that fetch and integrate domain-specific information into the model's knowledge base.

## Key Concepts  
- **Hallucinations**: Incorrect or misleading information generated by LLMs due to limited understanding of truth and verification mechanisms.  
- **Bias**: Systematic errors in training data leading to skewed outputs, often reflecting societal biases.  
- **Factual Accuracy**: The degree to which an LLM's responses are grounded in reality rather than mere word patterns.

## Mental Models  
- Use temperature tuning when you need more predictable but potentially less creative responses.  
- Think of retrievers as tools that ensure model outputs remain relevant and truthful by anchoring them in external knowledge.

## Anti-patterns  
- **Over-reliance on LLMs for Information Spread**: This can lead to misinformation, as LLMs may generate misleading content based on biased training data.
