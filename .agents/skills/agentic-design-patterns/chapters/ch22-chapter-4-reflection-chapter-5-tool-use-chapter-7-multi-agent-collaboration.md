## Core Idea
This chapter provides insights into reflection-based decision-making, tool utilization strategies, and effective multi-agent collaboration techniques.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Reflection Framework**: A structured approach to self-assessing decisions based on predefined criteria.
  - When to use: When evaluating the impact of past actions or decisions.
  - How: Identify key metrics (e.g., efficiency, effectiveness) and compare against benchmarks.

## Key Concepts
- **Affordance**: The perceived utility of a tool for a specific task.
- **Tool Mastery**: Achieving deep understanding and efficient application of tools in various contexts.
- **Nash Equilibrium**: A game-theoretic concept where no agent can benefit by changing their strategy while others remain unchanged.

## Mental Models
- Reflect on past decisions when <specific metrics are low>.

## Anti-patterns
- **Over-reliance on a single tool**: This approach fails when tools become obsolete or unsuitable for new challenges.
