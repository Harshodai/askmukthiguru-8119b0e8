# Chapter 16: Planning and Feedback in Agent Systems

## Core Idea
This chapter emphasizes the critical role of planning in creating autonomous thinking agents capable of solving complex problems by breaking them into manageable tasks.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Planning Loop**: A systematic approach to goal identification, task decomposition, execution, and result presentation.
  - When to use: For designing agents that require autonomy beyond simple interactions.
  - How: Follow the steps from identifying goals to presenting results through data collection, annotation, formatting, and reporting.

## Key Concepts
- **Feedback Mechanism**: Incorporates iterative refinement based on model outputs for enhanced accuracy.
- **Generic Planner**: A structured method adaptable across various LLM applications.

## Mental Models
- Use planning when designing autonomous agents that require problem-solving beyond basic interactions. Think of the Planning Loop as a foundational approach to achieving autonomy in agent design.

## Anti-patterns
- **Neglecting Feedback**: Avoid ignoring feedback loops, which can enhance model accuracy and reasoning.
- **Inadequate Planners**: Steer clear of systems without proper planner integration, leading to inefficient task handling.
