## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

Here is an alphabetical glossary of key terms based on the provided chapter titles:

- **Attention Mechanism** — A method used in neural networks to allow models to focus on specific parts of input data when making predictions or decisions. (Chapters 8, 9, 10)
  
- **Token Prediction** — The process by which a model predicts the next token in a sequence, commonly used in language modeling tasks. (Chapters 8, 9)

- **Overfitting** — A situation where a model performs well on training data but poorly on new, unseen data due to excessive complexity or lack of generalization. (Chapters 10, 12)

- **Underfitting** — A scenario where a model is too simple to capture the underlying patterns in the data, resulting in poor performance on both training and new data. (Chapters 10, 12)

- **Dropout** — A regularization technique that randomly deactivates neurons during training to prevent overfitting. (Chapters 9, 13)

- **Regularization** — Techniques used to prevent overfitting by adding constraints or modifying the model during training. (Chapters 9, 10)

- **Sequence Prediction** — The task of predicting the next element in a sequence, often involving recurrent neural networks (RNNs) or transformers. (Chapters 8, 9)

- **Language Model** — A type of machine learning model that learns the probability distribution of sequences of words for tasks like text generation. (Chapters 8, 12)

- **Minibatch Training** — The process of training a model by updating its parameters based on small subsets (minibatches) of the training data. (Chapters 5, 6)

- **Batch Training** — A method where the entire dataset is used to update a model's parameters in one pass or iteration. (Chapters 5, 6)

This glossary provides definitions and references to the chapters where each term is discussed.
